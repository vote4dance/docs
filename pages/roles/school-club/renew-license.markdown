# School or club renew license cycles

Renewal cycles are your season-transition control point. Done early, they prevent registration delays and eligibility gaps.

## What this step is for

You renew organization and member licensing coverage so all active dancers/teams are eligible for next-season events.

## Recommended timing

- Start planning: 45 days before season rollover
- Submit renewals: 30 days before rollover
- Final verification: at least 7 days before registrations open

## Step-by-step

### Step 1: Build renewal list

Create a list of:

- members/teams requiring renewal
- license items required per member type
- expected season year

### Step 2: Confirm federation season setup

Verify:

- new season item names/codes
- pricing updates
- approval model changes

Do not assume prior-season items are reused unchanged.

### Step 3: Submit organization-managed renewals

For each batch:

1. select correct season item
2. confirm member scope
3. submit application

Track application IDs for follow-up.

### Step 4: Complete payment and approval tracking

If paid model:

1. complete payment promptly
2. verify payment reconciliation

If manual approval:

1. monitor pending queue
2. escalate stalled approvals before cutoff

### Step 5: Verify active status before registrations

Before opening member registrations:

- all required renewals are Active
- no critical member remains pending/expired
- organizer has clean eligibility status view

## Common blockers and fixes

### "Wrong season selected"

Fix:

1. cancel/correct application quickly
2. reapply to correct season
3. confirm status after correction

### "Payments complete but members still pending"

Fix:

1. verify manual approval requirement
2. send application IDs to federation
3. avoid duplicate applications

### "Renewals started too late"

Fix:

1. prioritize competition-bound members
2. apply temporary internal hold on risky entries
3. communicate revised timelines to members

## What success looks like

The new season starts with active licensing coverage for required members and minimal emergency support workload.
