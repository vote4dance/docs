# Dancer age transfer

Age transfer means moving into a new age category because of federation season rules.

## What this step is for

This step helps dancers understand when an age-category change is expected and what to verify before entering new events.

## Who owns the decision

The federation owns the rule.

The school or club usually helps the dancer prepare the correct category.

The dancer should verify the outcome before registering.

## How it works

1. Check the federation age rules for the season.
2. Confirm that your birth-year or date information is correct.
3. Ask your club or federation if you are close to a category boundary.
4. Register in the category confirmed by the federation rules.
5. Review whether your future events and rankings now use the new age group.

## Why this matters

A wrong age group can affect:

- registration eligibility
- class placement
- rankings
- transfer decisions

## Common blockers

- The dancer assumes the previous season still applies.
- Birth data is missing or incorrect.
- The dancer enters an event before age transfer questions are resolved.

## What success looks like

The dancer competes in the correct age category with no last-minute correction needed.

## What to do in practice

- Ask before the season starts if you are close to a category boundary.
- Do not assume last year's age group still applies.
- If the event registration page shows a category that looks wrong, stop and ask before submitting.
