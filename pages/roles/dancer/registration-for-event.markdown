# Dancer registration for event

Registration is the point where the dancer enters a real competition flow and the platform checks whether the setup is complete enough to proceed.

## What this step is for

This step explains how a dancer enters an event and what may block registration.

## Who does this step

This may be done by:

- the dancer directly
- the school or club on behalf of the dancer
- the organizer on-site in exceptional cases

## Before you start

Confirm these first:

1. The event registration period is open.
2. Your license is active if the federation requires it.
3. Your school or club relationship is correct if that context is required.
4. You know which class or team you should enter.

## How it works

1. Find the correct event and registration period.
2. Choose the correct class or team context.
3. Fill the required participant details.
4. Confirm any required organization or license information.
5. Submit the registration.
6. Review the resulting status.

## What the system may check

- required participant fields
- registration window dates
- payment requirements
- license policy
- age eligibility
- class eligibility

## Common blockers

- Registration is already closed.
- The dancer selected the wrong class.
- A required license is missing or not active.
- Team or partner information is incomplete.

## What success looks like

The registration is submitted and accepted into the event workflow with no unresolved eligibility issue.

## If registration fails

Use this checklist:

1. Check whether registration is still open.
2. Check whether the correct class was selected.
3. Check whether the right license is active.
4. Check whether your partner, team, or organization information is complete.
5. Ask the organizer whether the event has additional manual approval rules.
