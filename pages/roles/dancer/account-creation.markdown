# Dancer account creation

This is the first real step in the dancer journey. If the account is wrong, every later step becomes slower and more manual.

## What this step is for

A dancer account stores your identity, federation affiliation, licensing status, registrations, and rankings in Vote4Dance. You must create this account yourself (personal, not shared).

## Where to go

Navigate to **https://vote4dance.com**

You'll see the Vote4Dance homepage with a **Sign Up** button in the top-right corner.

## Before you start

Gather these details:

- Your full name as your **federation or event organizer knows it** (example: "Alex Jordan Smith" — not nicknames)
- Your email address
- Your birth year (if your federation requires age verification; leave blank if unsure)
- Ideally, the name of the school/club you'll join (optional — you can add this later)

## Step-by-step: Create your account

### Step 1: Start the signup

1. Go to https://vote4dance.com
2. Click the blue **Sign Up** button (top-right corner)
3. You'll see a signup form with these fields:
   - Email
   - Password
   - Full Name
   - Birth Year (optional)

### Step 2: Fill in your account details

**Email** — Your permanent email address
- Example: `alex.smith@email.com`
- This is how you'll sign in
- **Make it a personal email** — not a shared school email

**Password** — At least 8 characters, mix of letters and numbers
- Example: `DanceChamp2025!`
- You'll use this to sign in every time
- Write it down securely

**Full Name** — Your name exactly as your federation records show it
- Example: `Alex Jordan Smith`
- NOT a nickname (the organizer won't find you if it's different)
- **Spelling matters** — Verify twice
- Use first and last name minimum

**Birth Year** (optional, but required by some federations)
- Example: `2008`
- Only enter the year if your federation requires age verification
- If you don't know, leave blank for now

### Step 3: Verify and submit

1. Review all fields (especially **Full Name** — spelling is critical)
2. Check "I agree to the terms" if shown
3. Click **Create Account** button
4. Wait a few seconds while the account is created

### Step 4: Verify your email

1. Check your email inbox for a message from Vote4Dance
2. Click the **Verify Email** link in that message
3. You're now fully signed up

## What your account looks like after creation

- You can sign in anytime at vote4dance.com with your email and password
- Your name appears in the top-right corner after signing in
- You see a dashboard with options like "Licenses", "Events", "My Rankings"
- **You cannot yet register for events** — you'll need a license (if required by your federation)

## Troubleshooting

### "The email is already in use"

This means a Vote4Dance account already exists with that email.

**Option 1:** Use a different email
- Create an account with a different email address

**Option 2:** Recover the existing account
- Click "Already have an account?" on the signup page
- Click "Forgot password?" and reset it
- Link to the existing account (better than creating a second one)

### "I don't see a verification email"

1. Wait 2 minutes
2. Check your spam/junk folder
3. If still missing, click "Resend verification email" on the signup page

### "Full Name shows but it's wrong"

**If the name is misspelled:**

1. Sign in to your account
2. Click your name in the top-right corner → **Profile**
3. Click **Edit** next to Full Name
4. Correct the spelling
5. Click **Save**

**Do this BEFORE** joining a school/club or applying for licenses (fixing it later is painful).

## What to check immediately after creation

- ✓ You can sign in again (try signing out, then signing back in)
- ✓ Your name is spelled correctly in your profile
- ✓ You only have ONE Vote4Dance account
- ✓ Your email address is active (you can sign in with it)

## Why accuracy matters

Your account may later be linked to:

- licenses
- school or club membership
- event registrations
- rankings and federation state

If the account data is inconsistent, support work becomes slower and more manual.

## Common blockers

- The dancer created multiple accounts.
- The name format does not match federation records.
- The account is created too late, after registration already opened.

## What success looks like

The dancer has one clear account that can be used for membership, licensing, registration, and ranking visibility.

## If something is wrong

- If you already have another Vote4Dance account, stop and decide which one should be kept.
- If your name or identity details are wrong, fix that before asking a school, club, or federation to connect anything.
- If the organizer or school cannot find you later, the most common cause is that the wrong account was used.
